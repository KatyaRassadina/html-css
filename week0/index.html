Let’s tell a story

It all started many years ago on a journey to Marrakech when I've met with an amazing artisan who sold me an incredible Berber pot from the Sahara region of Morocco.

The baskets are made of straw and wool and due to the artisanal nature of this product, each basket is completely unique and there may be the odd loose thread, but I think this adds to its charm.

Also I truly believe that buying these wooven pots I help to promote the fair trade. It is the best way to support the artisans, their family and to help them flourish in their endeavours, and perpetuate their ancestral skills.

My range of handwoven Berber Bread baskets, each with a carefully woven story has been hunted and collected by me from all over Morocco. Often with a moroccan style Tajine shaped top the berbers make gorgeous items for decorating in all sorts of different spaces or as an unusual storage option with loads of color.
 
A dazzling mix of vibrant contrasting colours and abstract designs create an unique individual one of a kind item. They are true eye catchers and add a beautifully authentic touch on a bookshelf, a table, or adorning a wall.

Spice up your room!

If you’re into the neon trend but you don’t want to turn your room into a 1980s cocktail bar, take a sharp left at Tribal; the two work together like pizza and beer. The tribal interiors trend is gaining ground and it’s one that’s going to stick around (our crystal ball says so).

Specifically, the tribal trend sees a focus on handmade Moroccan baskets either to store your laundry or spare cushions and throws (in the living room), kids toys and even as a relaxed container for your variety of indoor plants. Some of the coolest ones feature a chevron or geometric pattern with a bright colour or, alternatively, a soft pastel.
